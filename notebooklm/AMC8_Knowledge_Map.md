# AMC 8 Knowledge Map

## Exam profile
AMC 8 is a middle-school contest math exam. It emphasizes flexible problem solving more than routine schoolwork.

The most important content families are:
- arithmetic and percent/rate reasoning
- beginning algebra
- geometry and visual reasoning
- counting and probability
- graphs, tables, and data interpretation

## What students need to know

### 1. Arithmetic, ratios, and percent
Students should be comfortable with:
- fractions and mixed numbers
- ratios and rates
- percents and percent change
- unit conversions
- average, speed, and simple work/rate reasoning

Common failure modes:
- setting up the wrong ratio
- mixing part/whole with percent increase
- losing track of units

### 2. Beginning algebra
Students should be comfortable with:
- evaluating expressions
- one-variable equations
- pattern and sequence recognition
- translating short word problems into equations

Common failure modes:
- weak equation setup
- pattern guessing instead of rule finding
- careless arithmetic after correct setup

### 3. Geometry and spatial visualization
Students should be comfortable with:
- perimeter and area
- triangles, rectangles, circles, polygons
- Pythagorean theorem
- composite figures
- nets, solids, and basic 3D visualization

Common failure modes:
- misreading diagrams
- double-counting or missing a region
- using the wrong area/volume formula

### 4. Counting and probability
Students should be comfortable with:
- systematic listing
- simple arrangements and selections
- organized casework
- simple probability

Common failure modes:
- overcounting
- undercounting
- failing to separate cases cleanly

### 5. Graphs, tables, and data interpretation
Students should be comfortable with:
- reading bar charts and pie charts
- extracting values from tables
- converting visuals into equations or counts

Common failure modes:
- reading the picture but not the question
- copying values without combining them correctly

## Important techniques
- ratio reasoning
- estimation
- pattern finding
- diagram reading
- spatial visualization
- casework
- probability setup
- working backwards

## What "strong AMC 8 readiness" looks like
- easy arithmetic and geometry are stable
- medium counting/probability is not overwhelming
- students can read diagrams and tables accurately
- students can finish a short mixed set without collapsing on setup

## What usually needs repair first
- percent/rate confusion
- weak algebra setup
- poor diagram reading
- disorganized counting
