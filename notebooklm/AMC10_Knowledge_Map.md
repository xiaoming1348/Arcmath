# AMC 10 Knowledge Map

## Exam profile
AMC 10 covers early high-school contest math. It is broader and more structured than AMC 8.

Core content families:
- algebra and functions
- geometry and measurement
- elementary number theory
- counting and probability
- coordinate and word-problem modeling

AMC 10 does **not** include:
- trigonometry
- advanced algebra
- advanced geometry

## What students need to know

### 1. Algebra and functions
Students should be comfortable with:
- linear and quadratic equations
- expression manipulation
- factorization
- simple functional reasoning
- translating verbal conditions into algebra

Common failure modes:
- weak setup
- symbolic manipulation errors
- not recognizing structure in equations

### 2. Geometry and measurement
Students should be comfortable with:
- triangles, circles, polygons
- area and volume formulas
- similarity
- coordinate geometry
- geometric decomposition

Common failure modes:
- weak diagram modeling
- angle chasing errors
- not using symmetry or similar triangles

### 3. Number theory
Students should be comfortable with:
- divisibility
- factors and multiples
- remainders
- parity
- digit constraints

Common failure modes:
- jumping into brute force
- misunderstanding remainder conditions
- missing modular structure

### 4. Counting and probability
Students should be comfortable with:
- permutations and combinations
- organized casework
- probability setup
- complementary counting

Common failure modes:
- overcounting
- incomplete case splits
- confusing ordered and unordered choices

### 5. Coordinate and word-problem modeling
Students should be comfortable with:
- building equations from text
- coordinate geometry setup
- multi-step rate/work/mixture style modeling

Common failure modes:
- setting up the wrong variables
- not translating conditions cleanly
- solving the algebra but answering the wrong quantity

## Important techniques
- algebra setup
- equation solving
- function analysis
- diagram reading
- angle chasing
- coordinate modeling
- casework
- modular reasoning
- working backwards

## What "strong AMC 10 readiness" looks like
- easy and medium algebra are stable
- geometry does not collapse when the diagram is unfamiliar
- counting/probability setup is organized
- number theory problems are attacked structurally, not randomly

## What usually needs repair first
- algebra setup under time pressure
- diagram-driven geometry
- casework discipline
- modular/divisibility reasoning
