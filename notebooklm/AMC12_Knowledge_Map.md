# AMC 12 Knowledge Map

## Exam profile
AMC 12 covers the full high-school curriculum except calculus.

Core content families:
- advanced algebra and functions
- geometry and coordinate geometry
- trigonometry
- number theory
- counting and probability

Compared with AMC 10, AMC 12 expects students to be more comfortable with:
- trigonometry
- advanced algebraic structure
- more sophisticated geometry and combinatorics

## What students need to know

### 1. Advanced algebra and functions
Students should be comfortable with:
- polynomials
- systems and substitutions
- advanced equation structure
- function behavior and identities
- optimization through algebra

Common failure modes:
- missing hidden structure
- excessive computation instead of simplification
- weak function interpretation

### 2. Geometry and coordinate geometry
Students should be comfortable with:
- nontrivial diagram setup
- circles, tangencies, similarity
- transformations
- coordinate geometry and loci
- area/length optimization

Common failure modes:
- poor geometric modeling
- weak synthetic reasoning
- defaulting to coordinates when a cleaner approach exists

### 3. Trigonometry
Students should be comfortable with:
- sine, cosine, tangent
- angle relationships
- special angles
- trig-based inequalities/equations
- triangle trigonometry

Common failure modes:
- identity misuse
- not recognizing a trig structure early
- getting stuck in symbolic expansion

### 4. Number theory
Students should be comfortable with:
- modular arithmetic
- divisibility
- factorization
- integer constraints
- parity and digit reasoning

Common failure modes:
- trying random values
- not choosing the right modular viewpoint
- missing factor structure

### 5. Counting and probability
Students should be comfortable with:
- casework
- combinatorial structure
- probability setup
- symmetry
- inclusion-style counting

Common failure modes:
- chaotic casework
- counting the same object multiple times
- not reducing the problem before counting

## Important techniques
- function analysis
- optimization
- diagram reading
- angle chasing
- coordinate modeling
- trigonometric modeling
- modular reasoning
- casework
- symmetry

## What "strong AMC 12 readiness" looks like
- medium algebra and geometry are reliable
- at least medium trigonometry is stable
- hard counting/number theory does not feel completely foreign
- students can choose methods instead of forcing one style on every problem

## What usually needs repair first
- trig fluency
- advanced algebra setup
- hard combinatorics structure
- choosing an efficient method
