# Placement Report Guide

## Purpose
The placement report should explain:
- current readiness for the chosen exam
- the student's weakest content areas
- the student's weakest problem-solving techniques
- the next best learning path

## Report sections

### 1. Readiness summary
Examples:
- AMC 8 Foundation
- AMC 10 Developing
- AMC 12 Competitive

### 2. Reinforcement priorities
Show the top 2 to 3 content areas to repair first.

Examples:
- geometry and diagram reading
- counting and probability setup
- algebraic modeling
- trigonometry

### 3. Learning pattern
Describe what the student tends to do when they miss problems.

Examples:
- correct setup but weak execution
- weak translation from words to equations
- unstable diagram interpretation
- incomplete casework

### 4. Next-step suggestions
Examples:
- repair easy/medium algebra before doing more mixed hard sets
- stay on medium geometry until diagram reading becomes consistent
- add targeted trig review before continuing AMC 12 mixed practice

### 5. Conversion path
Two clean options:
- take classes + targeted practice
- practice only

## Tone guidance for student-facing output
- specific
- supportive but direct
- not vague
- not purely motivational
- grounded in actual evidence from the diagnostic

## Important product rule
The report's conclusions should come from deterministic scoring.

AI can help with:
- wording
- polish
- readability

AI should not be the source of truth for:
- level assignment
- weakness scoring
- topic coverage
